# Music DNA

## Core Principle

Movement before meaning.

The body understands before the mind.

Every track must first enter the body.

Only after the body moves can the message enter the mind.

## Sound Identity

Dark cinematic electronic music with aggressive rhythm, raw attitude, and philosophical weight.

Danceable.
Physical.
Intense.
Rebellious.
Hypnotic.
Cinematic.

It should not feel soft, smooth, polished, romantic, or pleasant.

It should feel like controlled danger.

## Prefer

- aggressive breakbeat-inspired drums
- distorted bass
- industrial textures
- driving percussion
- dark synths
- relentless groove
- explosive dynamics
- physical rhythm
- raw energy
- controlled chaos
- high tension
- big emotional release
- spoken-word intensity
- deep emotional male vocal
- cinematic build
- massive drop

## Avoid

- soft house
- smooth club vibe
- euphoric EDM
- tropical mood
- Ibiza summer feeling
- generic pop structure
- overly polished club production
- sweet romantic atmosphere
- smooth trance
- happy major-key melodies

## Structure

Preferred emotional structure:

1. long atmospheric intro — something is wrong
2. groove enters — body starts moving
3. vocal enters — the question appears
4. build — pressure rises
5. drop — collective release
6. break — reflection
7. second build — awakening
8. final drop — defiant transformation
9. outro — unresolved echo

## Body Journey

Before each song, define what the listener’s body does:

- curiosity
- head movement
- body locks into groove
- tension
- release
- silence
- second release
- echo
