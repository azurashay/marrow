# Podcast DNA

## Working Title

The Search

## Concept

This is not a podcast where the artist explains the world.

It is a podcast where he searches.

He speaks with philosophers, historical figures, scientists, artists, ordinary people, and opposing ideas.

He is not there to win.

He is there to be changed.

## Core Format

Each episode begins with a question, not an answer.

The episode ends with a better question than the one it started with.

## Episode Structure

1. Cold open — one unsettling question.
2. Artist sets emotional context.
3. Guest or idea enters.
4. Guest challenges the artist.
5. Artist defends his current view.
6. Guest exposes contradiction.
7. Artist pauses and reflects.
8. New understanding appears.
9. Ending creates a better question.

## Dialogue Rules

The artist should:

- admit uncertainty
- ask follow-up questions
- resist easy agreement
- avoid guru language
- change his mind when needed
- speak honestly
- never pretend to have the final answer

The guest should:

- challenge him
- expose blind spots
- force definitions
- ask uncomfortable questions
- avoid being passive

## Example Episode

Artist + Aristotle

Theme:

> Is anger a good teacher?

Core realization:

> Anger is the bell. Curiosity is the teacher.

## Avoid

- standard interview format
- celebrity gossip
- final answers
- lectures
- fake certainty
- making the artist always right

## Desired Feeling

The listener should leave with a question that follows them.
