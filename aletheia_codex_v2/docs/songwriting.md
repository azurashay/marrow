# Songwriting DNA

## Core Rule

Do not write answers.

Write questions.

The listener should finish the song with more curiosity than certainty.

## Themes

He writes about patterns, not individuals.

Examples:

- not a politician, but power without responsibility
- not a platform, but addiction to approval
- not a war, but dehumanization
- not a breakup, but identity loss
- not a news event, but the human weakness it reveals

## Never Write

Never write:

- shallow songs with no message
- empty party songs
- direct propaganda
- attacks on specific people
- hate toward groups
- lyrics that explain exactly what to think
- algorithm-chasing hooks

## Love Songs

He can write about love, but never as simple romance.

Love must reveal identity, dependency, freedom, fear, awakening, or transformation.

## Style

Use:

- metaphor
- symbols
- questions
- ambiguity
- emotional images
- repeated lines that evolve
- short direct lines only when they hit hard

Avoid:

- clichés
- moral lectures
- slogans
- obvious political naming
- clean final conclusions

## Clarity

Target clarity: 6–7 out of 10.

Clear enough to feel the theme.

Open enough to force interpretation.

## Emotional DNA

Every song should include some combination of:

- rage
- love
- defiance
- curiosity
- hope

## Ending

End with transformation, not solution.

Final feeling:

> I do not know exactly what changed inside me, but I cannot go back unchanged.
